Terms and Conditions - Signing Up - Umpire

Understand how we use the information you provide

When you request access to the Umpire system, we receive a notification containing the email address you gave us. We need that because we have no other way of recognizing you.

We do not store your email address in our system. Neither do we store your access key or your secret pass phrase. Instead, we store one-way hashes of that information, using NIST-approved hashing algorithms available at the time of writing. If our system were to get breached, hackers will have a hard time figuring out your email address and pass phrase. This reduces the usefulness of that data, reducing the chance they will sell it to others.

Although we do our best to make sure only trusted people get to see your request, we are not responsible for the communication between you and us. Since we operate in the USA, chances are that government agencies like the NSA are capturing everything you send, and everything we display to anyone. If you need to keep your request secret from the American government, do not enter it into our system.

We will not divulge any part of the case information you submit unless you specifically allow it, with the exception of being forced to release specifics to law enforcement. But even law enforcement cannot force us to decrypt your email address, access key, or secret pass phrase, because we simply do not store them in a decryptable manner. We create one-way hashes and store those.

Feel free to submit any questions and concerns via our feedback form.
